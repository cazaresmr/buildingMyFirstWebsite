My Statistics Website

1. Descriptive Statistics:

Descriptive statistics organizes and summarizes data, revealing key features. Measures like mean and median provide insights into data distribution, aiding effective communication in diverse fields, from business to the social sciences.

2. Inferential Statistics:

Inferential statistics extrapolates insights from sample data to broader populations. Hypothesis testing and regression analysis support decision-making, making it vital for scientific research, business analytics, and policy-making.

3. Probability:

Probability, foundational in statistics, quantifies event likelihood. Essential for modeling uncertainty, it underpins statistical reasoning, hypothesis testing, and decision-making in finance, engineering, and artificial intelligence.
